About


I am currently an undergraduate student pursuing a computer science degree at Johns Hopkins University. I’ve tutored students in math and computer science since high school, and as a student myself I know firsthand how it feels to be stumped by these daunting subjects. My struggles as a student and my experience as a tutor inspired me to create this website for other people who may find these subjects difficult to understand.

I have compiled class notes, textbooks, and online resources to create every lesson on this website. The goal behind these lessons is to provide a clear and concise explanation of concepts taught in a college course. Contrary to many classroom settings, learning should be exciting, interesting, and fun; therefore, I’ve attempted to make these lessons as engaging an interactive as possible through exercises, games, and quizzes.

Ultimately one may still find themselves confused no matter how the concepts are explained. Therefore, I have built in an online chat system that gives students the chance to ask for help and explain concepts to one another. Hopefully, this also builds a sense of community amongst users of this website and eliminates the frustration of learning by oneself.

I know how expensive online courses, coding bootcamps, or university can be, and thus I have made all lessons and features of this resources completely free.

I am always looking to improve the lesson and websites. If you would like to suggest a change, provide constructive criticism or have a business inquiry feel free to email me.
